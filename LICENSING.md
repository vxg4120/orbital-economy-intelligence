## Why AGPL-3.0

AGPL keeps this project genuinely open-source — auditable, forkable, and
credible as a public artifact — while being commercially unfriendly to
free-riders: anyone who runs a modified version as a network service must
publish their corresponding source under the same terms (AGPL §13). That
network-use provision is the whole point for a service-shaped project like this
one. As the sole copyright holder, I retain the right to **dual-license** the
same code commercially in the future; the AGPL grant to the public and a
separate commercial grant are not in tension.

## Contributions

Because relicensing (including future commercial dual-licensing) depends on
holding clear copyright, any future external contribution should be gated on a
**Contributor License Agreement (CLA)** assigning or licensing the contribution
back to the copyright holder. Please do not merge outside contributions without
one, or the relicensing option is lost.
